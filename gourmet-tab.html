Ambience Bengali Zamindari

Sonar Tori is a manifestation of the authentic Bengali culture. Not only will it gratify you with its rich Bengali food but also mesmerize you with its décor and view. The old rustic railings, ancient Bengali style bed, lamps, ‘haath pakhaa,’ tables made from old wooden windows and jewellery boxes, ‘kashar’ (brass) plates and cutleries will imbue in the atmosphere the charm of old Kolkata.

You can also choose to dine within the proximity of the Ganges and bask in the beauty of the surreal sunset being reflected in the river. The gentle fragrance of fresh flowers compliments the strong but enticing aroma of the exquisite Bengali Thali and the decorative ‘noukas’ take you back to your roots. The experience of dining here will impose upon you a renewed sense of sound, smell, taste, touch and a sight that is bound to etch this very view to memory!

Cuisine Bengali

The quintessential Bengali thali with all its glorious entrée in cheerful hues served here will connect your soul and stomach to the traditional Bengali roots that will have you savouring each delicacy. It will satiate your cravings with its wide variety of dishes ranging from the rich and spicy ‘Kosha Mangsho’, the subtle and soothing ‘Sorse Eelish Bhapey’ to the Chanar Payesh and Bhapa Sandesh which will not only overwhelm you with their sweetness but have you craving more with their distinct flavour!

The authentic Bengali thalis along with the a la carte services engage with every sensory organ as you walk through those doors to witness the plethora of delicacies sitting in a ginormous thali. Ambience Bengali

With an ambience and décor specific to rural Bengal, it embodies the roots of the delicacies it serves. The space is enwrapped in the charm of a rural hut with a haystack roof and mud textured walls which tempts you to hover your fingers over it. The walls are adorned with paintings depicting Bengal that adds character to the place alongside the carved wooden table and chairs. Every element magnifies the appeal of Bhoomi, taking you back to the vintage Bengal.

Cuisine Bengali

Bhoomi offers the best Bengali cuisine in town with authentic recipes coming down from generations, abundant in options for both vegetarians and non-vegetarians, ranging from Phulkopir Roast – which isn’t really roasted but a well-spiced cauliflower preparation shallow fried till crispy and served piping hot to go with the tempered chorchora dal, to kosha maghsho – a spicy lip smacking delicacy made of lamb or chicken with dry roasted spices in a thick brown gravy complimented by some steaming rice by the side, and these are just the tip of the ice berg because the menu has so much more to offer.

Signature Dish Bhapa Ilish

Bhapa iilish is one of the most sort after dishes in Bhoomi. Bhapa means ‘steamed’, and the dish is named so because the fish is marinated in mustard, yogurt, turmeric and other spices wrapped in banana leaf and steamed to perfection. The magic happens when it is served. It is like the inauguration of a very special item because you have got to untie the string which envelopes the banana leaf slowly unravelling it and that’s when you see the delicate ilish glistening golden in shorshe (mustard paste) with a chili on top. The flesh falls apart with a light touch and overwhelms your palate with the strong taste of mustard. Once you grapple with what just hit your taste buds you will start relishing every bite of the delectable golden fish.

Ambience Mediterranean

Set in the ambiance of the Mediterranean with golden and blue hues working its way through the restaurant, Dolce Vita is a visual delight. Arches and domes covering the space resemble the Mediterranean style, providing an opulence to the place and adding to its thematic experience. The polychromous hanging lights radiate a shine that arrests your attention and every element works in harmony to intensify the allure of this experience.

Cuisine Multi-cuisine

Standing by its name, Dolce Vita offers some of the best multi-cuisine buffets and a la carte services. Having an unbridled selection of cuisines, you can indulge in any of your favourites! The chilli mushroom which is a batter fried button mushroom with wok tossed spring onions, garlic chillies and spices that renders it crunchy yet soft and juicy in the middle is just the perfect starter for your meal. There is an endless variety to choose from whether starting your meal with mushroom 65 and ending it on a sweet note with flavoursome ice cream or the widely loved gulab jamun doused in sugar syrup. Dolce vita is here to cater to all your cuisine cravings so you can go back sated.

Signature Dish Lemon Fish

The signature dish of Dolce Vita when consumed will only have you wanting more of it. The fish is first seared on high heat providing that slight char and smokiness to the dish to add more character and then a buttery aromatic and refreshing sauce made of lemon, rosemary, butter and parsley is poured on top the fish. The lemon butter sauce richly complements the dish and takes it a level higher. The lemon fish is simple to make and yet so tasty.

Ambience Contemporary

Enveloped in a contemporary and aesthetic design, the restaurant sits on a white and black flooring with natural sunlight illuminating the room coming from the floor to ceiling glass panel windows. An open kitchen within a glass enclosure allows you see the precision and care with which the sumptuous dishes are created by the chefs for you to be assured of its quality. The wall covered with shelves stores artistic pots attracts the eyes while the avant-garde design of the restaurant keeps you enamoured in the aura it emanates.

Cuisine Multi-cuisine

This restaurant sits on the lap of one of the most luxurious resorts in west Bengal, serving the best multi-cuisine dishes. It takes you on a cruise in the sea of delicacies covering the longitudes of the world with its English breakfast, Indian lunch, pan-Asian snacks and Italian dinner menu. With the rising sun you get served sunny side up eggs, baked beans and breads, by noon your nostrils are going to catch the fragrance of the Indian spices emitting out of the tandoori murgh, and by night you can experience spaghetti arrabbiata cooked in tomato and basil sauce. Viva takes your palate on a much desired vacation, treating it with the best of the world cuisines.
