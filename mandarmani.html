Mandarmani

Mandarmani beckons its visitors to experience the sandy shores, the water sports and the serenity of the azure waters.

A long stretch of the sandy beach with coconut groves all around, the seamless sea and thrilling water sports are what Mandarmani has to offer. The serene atmosphere of the sea and the seemingly unending stretch of one of the longest motorable beaches in Mainland Asia abounding in venturous activities will have a rush of adrenaline pumping into your veins and liberate you by imbuing solace into your soul. A tranquil seaside resort village in West Bengal, Mandarmani gives you a chance to enjoy unrestrained tranquillity and solitude and the perfect respite from the chaos and din of city life.

Trivia

The sight of red crabs crawling alongside the beach made it appear as a shimmering field full of red flowers which appealed to the imagination of the residents who initially named it ‘Mandar’, and later on changed its name to Mandarmani.

Indulgence

What must be done while you’re in Mandarmani is to experience the beach and the eternal waters in all their glory while you indulge in gratuitous water sports and fill your stomach with desired delights till the exhaustion of the city life wears out of you and all that remains is an ecstatic joy that never wears off!

Best time to visit

Monsoon conjures the ideal atmosphere and the beach looks ethereal. It's the peak season here as it offers a mesmerizing view of the beach and the rain. The sight is spectacular and accommodations are sparse. However, the aesthetically appealing view attracts people from all over. Cotton clothes and swimwear are advisable to wear in Mandarmani. It is mandatory to carry an umbrella and sunscreen to avoid sunburn. Appropriate clothes will only help you to achieve optimum enjoyment and relaxation. Winter the next best season to visit with the ‘Surf n Sand’ festival gathering many here.

Mandarmani Beach

Mandarmani, West Bengal 721455

Situated in the East Midnapore district of West Bengal, Mandarmani is estimated to have the longest motorable beach in India, around 13 km long. The most quirky aspect of this beachside is the sight of red crabs crawling alongside the beach, making it look like a shimmering field full of red flowers. This appeals to the imagination of the residents. Formerly known as Mandar, it, later on, changed its name to Mandarboni and then Mandarmani. A hit as a holiday destination amongst tourists, the serene tropical seaside breeze here, surely melts hearts and refreshes minds.

Mandarmani embodies a mixture of both tradition and modernity. In fact, it contains a bit of bohemian Bengali culture -an earnest, simple yet relaxed lifestyle led by the fishing communities.
