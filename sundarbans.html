Sundarbans

Feast your eyes on serene expanses of green forests and vast azure waters at the isles of Sundarbans. The largest mangrove forest of the world is a resplendent sight with its majestic flora and fauna, as well as the raw appeal of the untouched hinterlands. The Sundarbans, adorning the southern stretch of West Bengal tempts you with its unadulterated natural bounty that soothes your weary soul. The Sunderban National Park boasts multiple accolades as a Tiger Reserve, a biosphere, a UNESCO world heritage site, and as the abode of a mesmerizing range of wildlife, from the most popular Royal Bengal tiger to innumerable birds and reptiles. Named after the “Sundari” trees that encircle the dusky islands, Sundarbans has been charmingly dubbed the “beautiful forest” and you can discover why!

It is a land of tranquil beauty where the Gangetic delta melds languorously into the Bay. Find yourself on a boat in the Sundarbans, following the currents, passing by springs, creeks, and estuaries, flanked by thick, lush greenery. With over 400 tigers on record and several thousand deer, it would be no surprise to spot a striped tail or an amber eye in the dark! But the Sundarbans is a mecca not just for the thrill-seeking way-farers; its status as a reserve ensures the presence of several endangered species like King Crabs or the Olive Ridley turtle that can be found sunning on sandy shores. Choosing Sundarbans as a travel destination invites you to explore sanctuaries and watchtowers, to enjoy boat safaris on the river. With its inimical balance of exciting and relaxing attractions, Sundarbans will check off every requirement on your travel checklist into the wild.

Trivia

It is an unbelievable fact that the Sundarbans of India cover an area that is ten times the size of Venice! Apart from the Tiger Project, the deltas also rose to global attention as the terrifyingly real setting of Amitav Ghose's award-winning novel, The Hungry Tide.

Indulgence

The open skies and endless babble of the river water will completely immerse you into the life of the idyllic forest in the Sundarbans. Simple pleasures coalesce into a poignantly memorable stay for travellers here. Whether you are a tourist looking for a moment of well-earned reprieve, a nature enthusiast eager to observe the diverse flora and fauna of the land, or a thrill chasing adventurer intent on catching a glimpse of the Royal Bengal tiger, Sundarbans has much to offer that can turn the memories your journey into a story as gripping as the folklore abounding in these lands.

Best time to travel

The months from October to March constitute the peak tourist season in the Sundarbans. It is essentially winter, a pleasant period when the temperatures are comfortable enough for you to explore the delta at large. It is also the ideal time to view migratory birds, or Olive Ridley turtles and crocodiles. Summer is considered the best time for safaris as wildlife can often be found quenching their thirst in open ponds. Note that the weather then is usually overly hot and humid. Monsoons make the forests lush, bright, and delightfully green but the rains also disrupt travel and cause floods so tourists are generally discouraged to choose this time to visit. Overall, the relatively mild and dry winters are certainly your best bet for a relaxing and enjoyable trip into the mangrove delta!

Sajnekhali bird Sanctuary

Sajnekhali | West Bengal 743370

Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary is where you can listen to the natural concert of sweet-throated birds whose melodious music echoes throughout the Sundarbans. As opposed to the somber thrill of the tiger reserve the bird sanctuary is joyfully cacophonous, especially in migratory season. Bird watchers are advised to keep binoculars and lenses handy as birds, of both native and foreign species, can be found nestled in branches hiding with skillful camouflage or soaring in swift flight between trees or over water. Species to spot include egrets, herons, kingfishers, sandpipers, plovers, white-bellied sea eagles, whimbrels, curfews, and lapwings; the sanctuary also houses a breeding colony for herons. At Sajnekhali, you will find a well-appointed Mangrove Interpretation centre that contains details of the extant plant species there and a visitor's centre which has a crocodile enclosure and a shark pond.

The legacy of the Sundarbans is interestingly intertwined with that of India from its very inception. You will find traces of its history in the form of scattered ruins. The history of the Sundarbans makes for an interesting read as you will recall how lands were controlled during the Mughal period. Until the nineteenth century when the British mapped the landscape, large parts of the forest remained uncharted, unexplored, and enshrouded in woody darkness, large parts of it were declared as reserve forests. Slowly the Sundarbans you know today emerged as a wildlife sanctuary in 1977 and then as a national park in 1984. Today you can boast about visiting a world heritage site as declared by UNESCO and happily look forward to spotting Royal Bengal Tigers behind the billowy curtains of mangroves!

Festivals & Culture

Sundarbans | West Bengal 743370

The event which you would love to experience in the Sundarbans is the vibrant Bonbibi Pala or the drama of Bonbibi celebrated during March, centering around the revered goddess. The dramatic performance of the myth of Bonbibi showcases the eclectic folk culture of this diversely populated delta. Dance and music performances by local artists make for a lively ambiance that gives you an intimate glimpse of the lives of these hardworking and courageous people. The festival thus serves the dual purpose of worshipping the goddess and requesting her benevolent protection for the honey collectors or fishermen, as well as to uphold age-old tradition and ritual that give you amazing moments to remember the culture of Sundarbans.

The backwaters of the Sundarbans are well known for seafood due to the abundance of water around. Fish, prawns, and even crabs are freshly caught from the waters of the bay and are resplendently presented in a variety of delicious recipes. Summer

Summers in Sunderban are mostly extremely hot and temperatures can soar from 20 -42 degrees Celsius. Locals are plagued by heatwaves and the paucity of drinkable water as ponds and estuaries run dry. These adverse circumstances make tourism difficult for all but tenacious wildlife lovers. The summers do force animals to venture out in search of water, crocodiles and crabs lay drowsily in the muddy cold salt pools so if you can brave the heat and discomfort you may catch some memorable shots on your cameras.

Monsoon

Monsoons straddle the line between disaster and rejuvenation for the delta. With the rising water level and the threat of submerging islands, heavy rains are feared by the locals. The river usually overflows which makes travel, for work or travel, next to impossible, and access to the wildlife parks and sanctuaries is completely shut off. The difficulty in commerce makes this a rough time for the inhabitants who are dependent on tourism or forest products for their livelihood. On the other hand, this is a time when nature rejoices as temperatures dip down to 25-30 degrees Celsius. The mangroves and other flora grow abundantly lush and thick and the wildlife also enters their breeding season.

Winter

Winters are the welcome reprieve after the heat and humidity of summer and monsoon. The temperatures are at a comfortable 10-30 degrees Celsius with nights being colder. The parks are open as animals and birds are prone to roam more freely. You should choose this time to best enjoy travels by boat as the river is gentle and calming. The cool breeze carrying missives of the wildlife that surrounds you is refreshing and invigorating so you feel enthused to participate in all the activities from safaris to bird-watching or simply relax at your accommodation with a gorgeous view of the mangroves and rivers.

Airport - NSCB International Airport

The airport nearest to the Sundarbans is the Netaji Subhash Chandra Airport in Kolkata, the capital city of West Bengal. The airport is a hub of lively activity connected to all major cities of India and international cities as well. You can fly in from any corner of the country and beyond to visit the majestic delta in comfort. Cars for hire and tourist buses are available from the airport that reach the Sundarbans by road in about three hours.

Local Transport - Island to Island : Motor boats, Launches. Market area : Rickshaws, Vans

Riverine waterways are the exciting and exceptional feature of transport in Sundarbans. Since the Sundarbans are spread across more than fifty islands the only way for you to reach them is by water. It makes for a memorable experience as you travel from one tourist spot to another by motorboats or launches which are specially designed to be extremely quiet to avoid spooking the wild animals. You can observe the rows of mangroves from a shaded deck, and spot a tiger striding past at any point! The boats also take you close to banks where crocodiles or turtles rest and you may even find the bumpy hide of a reptile right next to your boat's hull.
