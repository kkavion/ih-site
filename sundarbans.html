Sundarbans

With meticulous planning, we wish to tailor holidays just for you.

Feast your eyes on serene expanses of green forests and vast azure waters at the isles of Sundarbans. The largest mangrove forest in the world, Sundarbans adorning the southern stretch of West Bengal tempts you with its unadulterated natural bounty that soothes your weary soul. The Sunderban National Park boasts multiple accolades as a Tiger Reserve, a biosphere, a UNESCO world heritage site, and as the abode of a mesmerizing range of wildlife, from the popular Royal Bengal tiger to innumerable birds and reptiles. Named after the “Sundari” trees that encircle the dusky islands, Sundarbans has been charmingly dubbed the “beautiful forest” and you can discover why!

Trivia

It is an unbelievable fact that the Sundarbans of India cover an area that is ten times the size of Venice!

Indulgence

Sundarbans is a place that will tempt and prompt you to explore. The boats sail deep into creeks until you are close enough to touch land. To sit with cameras poised, eyes peeled and ears cocked, and suddenly spot a wild animal just carelessly appearing before you will uniquely combine the backwater experience commendable with that of the forest.

Best time to travel

The winter months from October to March constitute the peak tourist season when the temperatures are comfortable enough for you to explore the delta at large. It is also the ideal time to view migratory birds, or Olive Ridley turtles and crocodiles. Summer is considered the best time for safaris as wildlife can often be found quenching their thirst in open ponds.

Monsoon 2,450 sq ft

Monsoons straddle the line between disaster and rejuvenation for the delta. With the rising water level and the threat of submerging islands, heavy rains are feared by the locals. Good To Know

This is a time when nature rejoices as temperatures dip down to 25-30 degrees Celsius. The mangroves and other flora grow abundantly lush and thick and the wildlife also enters their breeding season.
