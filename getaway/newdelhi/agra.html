Agra

A mystical state, Agra teleports you to a glorious past embellished with the ever-enchanting Taj Mahal and fascinating legacy of forts, tombs and mausoleums.

Despite the crowds of people hovering to get a glimpse of the Taj Mahal, you will find yourself drawn to its magnificent beauty. Crowned with the sparkle of brilliance, the Taj Mahal is a year-round attraction for tourists internationally. Behind the musk of the red valour of the Red Fort and veiled by the tranquil shades of the tombs and mosques were the dust and decrepitude of negligence. The Mughal kings took up the responsibility of its creation. Flavoured by the sweet delight of Petha and splashed with the bustling joy of street shopping, this city of antiquity is the romantic ruins of the Mughal dynasty.

Trivia

Agra is home to the three UNESCO heritage sites of Taj Mahal, Red Fort and Fatehpur Sikri. It is also the second largest exporter of footwear in the world so be sure to pick up some pairs when here!

Best time to visit

The best time to visit would be between November to March, which is also the travel season. You might find a bit of a crowd visiting at this time to hear Agra's testimony and see its elegance. The cool weather will allow you to spend a lot of time under the sun and gazing at the jewels of the Mughal Empire.

Indulgence

Admiring the Taj Mahal should be on the top of your list when in Agra. Explore the beauties of the Mughal era, especially the Fatehpur Sikri, Red Fort, Itimad-ud-daulah's Tomb and Akbar's Tomb to fall in love with its charm. Imbibe the religious essence of Jama Masjid, witness the Kalakriti Dance Drama Show and relax at the Dolphin Water Park.

Though Agra has a semiarid climate, the climatic changes have made the weather extreme. In summer you will need sunscreen to avoid getting severely tanned if you are travelling by local commute or by bike. In winter there is a biting chill so you will need to carry socks, coats and sweaters. The rainy season sees flooded roads. However, carrying lotions and an umbrella will allow you a pleasant exploration.

Summer March - May

You can wear the short skirt or quarter pants that you bought to show off your summer-shredded body during this season. The Delhi crowd likes fashion and does not judge people by their eccentricity so you can sport your fashion sense in this climate. The temperature outside is dry and hot so it's better to be indoors or in air-conditioned places.

Good To Know

If you need to go outside use UV protection sunglasses, sunscreen and an umbrella. Carry a bottle of water to stay hydrated as well.

Good To Know

Airway Agra Airport

Agra Airport has both national and international flight services. You can avail of this opportunity for fast and comfortable interstate or foreign travel. The security check is tight so you should be there at the check-in 15 to 20 minutes earlier than the time specified on your flight ticket. You can book your ticket online from their official website or by going to any local travel agent.

Railway Agra Fort, Agra Cantt

Agra Fort and Agra Cantonment are the popular railway stations from where you can book your railway ticket either on the spot at the counter or online. The prices of railway transport are cheap and the speed is sufficiently fast so it's a good option if you're traveling either between states or to nearby places. The Taj Express and Shatabdi running from here have the best accommodation and travel the fastest. It also has good connectivity to other metropolitan cities.

Local Transport Rental

For travelling by bus you could take the UPSRTC or Uttar Pradesh State road transportation Corporation. These buses stop at most of the popular tourist destinations like the Taj Mahal and Red Fort since the bus stops are usually located there. Even the local people might guide you if you ask for assistance. The bus numbers, as well as the names of the places that they will stop at, are written on the bus itself. It is also announced by the conductor of the bus at the bus stop.

FAQs

Why should I visit Agra?

Opposed to popular belief, you should visit the Agra to experience the rugged forest life and not just to spot the famed royal Bengal tigers. Though there are about 96 of them including cubs, there haven't been many regular sightings which is why it can be quite disappointing if you spend your entire visit fixated on the same. On the contrary, cruising the delta and taking morning boat rides in the narrow canals will allow you a surreal experience of nature by spotting rare species of medicinal plants, the charming Sundari trees all around and rare birds and animals of the wild. Visit the delta for itself and let the tigers be a bonus to your thrilling trip here.

Is it safe to travel to Agra?

Yes, it is safe provided you stay within the protected areas of the forest. Travelling in small groups will allow you to venture deeper into the forest where bigger boats and cruises cannot enter. However, venturing on your own is very dangerous as the forest is impenetrable in some parts that are better left untraversed without supervision and special protection.

Where to stay in Agra?

The stay at Agra is sure to add to your forest experience. If you enjoy the thrill of camping then you should opt for camps beside watchtowers or resorts which provide camping facilities. If you prefer to have a more relaxed vacation and enjoy luxuries in the mangrove forest then the Tiger Camp Resort, Hotel Sonar Bangla, Tiger Roar Resort and Tora Eco Resort are options that will delight you.

How long does it take to tour Agra and what are the top sites to visit there?

To have a relaxed tour of the mangrove forest and visit all the popular sites would take about 3 to 4 days. Some of the top sites to be visited are the Agra National Park, Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary, Sudhanyakhali and Sajnekhali Watch Towers, Bhagabatpur Crocodile Project, Netidhopani and Kanak Islands among others. Crocodiles, birds, an age-old temple and the ferocious tigers can all be found in these places along with sightings of more of the wild.

What should be carried while travelling to Agra?

You must carry your Id proof while travelling to Agra. Cash in small denominations should also be taken as there are no ATMs inside the forest. Mosquito repellants and other ointments that will keep the insects at bay along with comfortable clothes suitable for the weather you will be travelling in are also advisable to carry to have the desired forest experience.

How to travel to Agra from Kolkata?

You can begin your journey from Kolkata at Sealdah Station. The train will take you to Canning in about 1 hour 30 minutes. From this point on there are multiple routes you can take to get to the isles of Agra. A bus or car can be taken from Canning to Godakhali, Sonakhali, Koikhali or Jharkhali depending on where you have chosen to stay for your trip. From the ferry ghat of these spots, you need to take a motor boat or launch to venture into the heart of the mangrove forest. If you're more comfortable travelling by bus then you can also board one from Esplanade to reach Canning and follow the same route from then onward. Since the Agra mainly consist of a set of islands, one of which you'll be staying at, riverine transport is the only way to travel around the place which has a unique thrill of its own.
