Parwanoo

Nestled in the foothills of the Shivaliks, Parwanoo calls you to intimately experience the bounties of nature.

A striking hill station on the borders of Haryana and Himachal Pradesh, Parwanoo is a town surrounded by small hills. If you are on the lookout for a fun road trip with friends or a weekend getaway with your partner, the rolling green hills and the wide sky above along with the intimacy of a small scenic town can provide you with all the comfort and adventure you desired. You can go trekking, ride cable cars and stroll through apple orchards. The also town provides some breathtaking views from high atop the hills and allows one to bask in the vastness of the landscape.

Indulgence

Take the famous Timber Trail Ride and observe the steps cut along the hills, the hidden peaks and the scenic Kaushalaya Valley. Visit the Cactus Garden at Punchakula anytime during April to September, pack jars of interesting mixes of jams and jellies made from instantly-picked fruits and snack on the meat pickles sold at the Malli shop.

Best time to visit

The breezy summers are ideal for mountain climbing and trekking and make the scenic charm of Parwanoo all the more picturesque. In winter the chilly atmosphere gives the place a misty air and sets the perfect mood for a bonfire or simply snuggling in your hotel room with a fantastic view and some great food to eat.

Trivia

From a tiny sleepy village, Parwanoo has become a flourishing industrial town after Himachal Pradesh attained its statehood.

Renuka Lake

Sirmaur district, Himachal Pradesh

Situated about 672km above sea level, Renuka Lake is considered the largest in the state with an abounding ecosystem of beautiful flora and fauna surrounding it. Though to be the birthplace of Lord Parashuram, several pilgrims visit here during the months of October to November to celebrate the auspicious five-day festival of Sri Renuka Ji to witness the divine unification of Parashuram and his mother Renuka.

Good To Know: Tourists often visit the lake for picnics, boat rides, lion safaris and a visit to the zoo.

Pinjore Garden

Ambala, Kalka - Shimla Rd, Pinjore, Haryana 134101

Famous for its 17th Century gardens overlooking the Shivalik Hills, Pinjore houses a mini zoo, a Japanese-style garden, a nursery, and many pleasant picnic spots. A central waterway forms the skeleton of the garden dotted with flowering plants on either side. The Bhima Devi Temple accompanying it makes it religiously sacred. Pinjore provides a dynamic experience of tourism to its visitors, unique to itself.

Good To Know: The 8th-century temple is now an open-air museum depicting statues of ancient deities in exotic postures.

Gurkha Fort

Subathu, Himachal Pradesh 173206

Gorkha Fort situated on Subathu Hill had protected our people against the invading British Army. Apart from the Gorkha Museum which exhibits artefacts and objects from 1815, the main fort lies unprotected and unrestored, the bricked wall has creepers growing along. Cooped under one of Parwanoo’s extensive forest cover, the Fort lives on to inspire people.

Good To Know: Tourists flock to witness nature and time taking over a man-made structure in its grandest manner, signifying harmony over war.

Dagshai

Himachal Pradesh 173210

5689 ft above sea level, Dagshai provides an almost unrealistic view of Panchkula and Chandigarh city lights. The town has a haunted essence for it has witnessed several executions and tortures in the Central jail during the British Raj. The Dagsahi Central Jail Museum is the only one of the two jail museums in the country and is also used as an office under the Military Engineering Service.

Good To Know: The name comes from the Daagh-e-Shahi mark upon a criminal's forehead that would banish them from this village.

Kali Mata Temple

Sohan Nagar, Kalka, Haryana 133302

Pilgrims come to this temple dedicated to the goddess Kali in large numbers and offer garlands, incense, and chunnis. It is believed that the Pandavas had visited this spot during the Agyatva phase of their exile and had built a shrine for the Goddess. Since then the surrounding region of Kalka is known for its holy significance.

Good To Know: It is crowded during Navratri in the months of September to October as the goddess Kali is worshipped as the ultimate destroyer of all evil.

Gurudwara Nada Sahib

Nada sahib, Panchkula, Haryana 134109

Guru Gobind Singh Ji had halted here in 1688 on his visit to Anandpur Sahib after the Battle of Bhangani. Upon this blessed ground a Gurudwara was built, however, the original structure was soon replaced by a two-story dome with a large spacious brick courtyard. Accompanied by a Museum, the Gurudwara also stands to educate and inform of its rich history.

Good To Know: Devotees and pilgrims are fed langars daily and on full moon days, the halls are packed for religious prayers and community meals.

FAQs

Which is a better place to visit, Old Digha or New Digha?

The choice really does lie with the visitor because preferences matter. While Old Digha’s spaced-out hotels and calm shores are perfect for those on a honeymoon, New Digha’s sandy shores, proximity to the park and aquarium and star-rated hotels make it frequently visited as well. Since they’re only 3kms apart, it’s possible to experience both the calm and the bustle.

Which are the top beaches of Digha?

The Old and New Digha beaches along with Udaipur and Shankarpur Beaches are some of the cleanest in West Bengal and offer a charm of their own. Whether you seek seclusion in the waters in a romantic getaway or the classic sandy shores, both can be found on these beaches. The food stalls and water sports available only make the whole experience more exciting.

Are water sports available in Digha?

They are available in abundance on all the beaches, especially the most frequented New Digha Beach. The stalls provide you with equipment and assistance for any activity of your choice. You can be sure to have your enjoyment multiplied with the dash of adrenaline this will bring you. Wearing suitable clothes that won’t wear you down with their weight once wet or fly about if you’re in the air is highly advisable as that makes the experience all the more pleasant.

Which are the best places to eat in Digha?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

What is the fastest route to Digha from Kolkata?

The fastest way to travel to Digha is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 33 minutes to get there but the travel can be a bit pricey, ranging from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000. Cheaper options will take you longer to reach Digha but the journey will be equally exciting if you’re surrounded by your loved ones. You can take a train from Howrah Station and reach in 3 hours 25 minutes, tickets will be priced anywhere between Rs 150 and Rs 850. Buses from Esplanade will take you there in 4 hours 15 minutes and can charge you Rs 150 to Rs 350.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
