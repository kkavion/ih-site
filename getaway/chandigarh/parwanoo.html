Parwanoo

Nestled in the foothills of the Shivaliks, Parwanoo calls you to intimately experience the bounties of nature.

A striking hill station on the borders of Haryana and Himachal Pradesh, Parwanoo is a town surrounded by small hills. If you are on the lookout for a fun road trip with friends or a weekend getaway with your partner, the rolling green hills and the wide sky above along with the intimacy of a small scenic town can provide you with all the comfort and adventure you desired. You can go trekking, ride cable cars and stroll through apple orchards. The also town provides some breathtaking views from high atop the hills and allows one to bask in the vastness of the landscape.

Trivia

From a tiny sleepy village, Parwanoo has become a flourishing industrial town after Himachal Pradesh attained its statehood.

Best time to visit

The breezy summers are ideal for mountain climbing and trekking and make the scenic charm of Parwanoo all the more picturesque. In winter the chilly atmosphere gives the place a misty air and sets the perfect mood for a bonfire or simply snuggling in your hotel room with a fantastic view and some great food to eat.

Indulgence

Take the famous Timber Trail Ride and observe the steps cut along the hills, the hidden peaks and the scenic Kaushalaya Valley. Visit the Cactus Garden at Punchakula anytime during April to September, pack jars of interesting mixes of jams and jellies made from instantly-picked fruits and snack on the meat pickles sold at the Malli shop.

Summer March - May

Summers stretch from March to May and are usually breezy and delightful. The pleasurable air enhances the scenic beauty and makes it the perfect season for adventures like mountain climbing and trekking. Since it is followed by spring, the trees are abundant with flowers and fruits and the fields are filled with yield.

Good To Know
The festival of Bishu and Basant Panchami is also celebrated during this time making it ideal for tourism.

Monsoon June - September

The Monsoon season lasts from June to September and every year the region witnesses a heavy torrential downpour. Due to heavy soil erosions, landslides are extremely prevalent on highways and can cause severe accidents.

Good To Know
Despite the step farming and strong plantations all along the hills, the rains still cause a chaotic situation every year and it is inadvisable to visit Parwanoo at this time.

Winter November - January

Winters in Parwanoo last from October to February and temperatures can range from sub-zero to 10 degrees Celsius. With heavy misty clouds and dense fog, the place acquires a magical aura about itself. It is the perfect time to cuddle in your hotel bedroom while enjoying a stunning view with a cup of coffee or simply enjoy a comfortable afternoon basking in the winter sun in the orchards with children playing around you.

Good To Know
It is advisable to carry loads of warm clothes and to drink warm beverages to keep oneself heated.

Railway Kalka Railway Station

Trains can be availed from the Chandigarh Junction Railway station to Kalka from where Parwanoo is just 4 km away. One can also avail trains from Solan which is 40kms away. The trains bound for Shimla from Kalka pass through the Taksal railway Station situated within Parwanoo. It is considered to be a heritage rail line passing making travel easier for travellers. If you board a non-ac bogie, the experience of being carried upon the hills is quite joyous.

Roadway Parwanoo Bus Stand

Parwanoo is best reached by road as it is a popular road trip destination Buses are frequently and easily available from neighbouring cities like Delhi, Haryana, Chandigarh, Ambala, and Punjab. The town falls on the Chandigarh-Shimla highway of NH22 as one passes Panchkula on its way. Once you have reached MDR 6, Parwanoo can be easily reached shortly. The winding mountain roads and the gradually appearing silhouettes of hills in the background can give a pleasant experience to your trip be it by a bus or by your private car.

Local Transport Rental

Transportation within the city is mostly available through cars that can be easily hired for sightseeing throughout the day. A private car or taxi will tour you through the top 10 visiting spots and some agencies even provide cars for sightseeing in neighbouring towns and cities. However, even though long distances cannot be covered on foot, the best way to get around in Parwanoo is to take a walk along the hilly trails of the slopping streets. You can interact with the locals, take simple street photographs, and explore every corner of the town.

FAQs

Why should I visit Parwanoo?

Opposed to popular belief, you should visit the Parwanoo to experience the rugged forest life and not just to spot the famed royal Bengal tigers. Though there are about 96 of them including cubs, there haven't been many regular sightings which is why it can be quite disappointing if you spend your entire visit fixated on the same. On the contrary, cruising the delta and taking morning boat rides in the narrow canals will allow you a surreal experience of nature by spotting rare species of medicinal plants, the charming Sundari trees all around and rare birds and animals of the wild. Visit the delta for itself and let the tigers be a bonus to your thrilling trip here.

Is it safe to travel to Parwanoo?

Yes, it is safe provided you stay within the protected areas of the forest. Travelling in small groups will allow you to venture deeper into the forest where bigger boats and cruises cannot enter. However, venturing on your own is very dangerous as the forest is impenetrable in some parts that are better left untraversed without supervision and special protection.

Where to stay in Parwanoo?

The stay at Parwanoo is sure to add to your forest experience. If you enjoy the thrill of camping then you should opt for camps beside watchtowers or resorts which provide camping facilities. If you prefer to have a more relaxed vacation and enjoy luxuries in the mangrove forest then the Tiger Camp Resort, Hotel Sonar Bangla, Tiger Roar Resort and Tora Eco Resort are options that will delight you.

How long does it take to tour Parwanoo and what are the top sites to visit there?

To have a relaxed tour of the mangrove forest and visit all the popular sites would take about 3 to 4 days. Some of the top sites to be visited are the Sundarban National Park, Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary, Sudhanyakhali and Sajnekhali Watch Towers, Bhagabatpur Crocodile Project, Netidhopani and Kanak Islands among others. Crocodiles, birds, an age-old temple and the ferocious tigers can all be found in these places along with sightings of more of the wild.

What should be carried while travelling to Parwanoo?

You must carry your Id proof while travelling to Parwanoo. Cash in small denominations should also be taken as there are no ATMs inside the forest. Mosquito repellants and other ointments that will keep the insects at bay along with comfortable clothes suitable for the weather you will be travelling in are also advisable to carry to have the desired forest experience.

How to travel to Parwanoo from Kolkata?

You can begin your journey from Kolkata at Sealdah Station. The train will take you to Canning in about 1 hour 30 minutes. From this point on there are multiple routes you can take to get to the isles of Parwanoo. A bus or car can be taken from Canning to Godakhali, Sonakhali, Koikhali or Jharkhali depending on where you have chosen to stay for your trip. From the ferry ghat of these spots, you need to take a motor boat or launch to venture into the heart of the mangrove forest. If you're more comfortable travelling by bus then you can also board one from Esplanade to reach Canning and follow the same route from then onward. Since the Parwanoo mainly consist of a set of islands, one of which you'll be staying at, riverine transport is the only way to travel around the place which has a unique thrill of its own.
