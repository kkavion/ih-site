Kasauli

Enchant yourself with Kasauli’s tall rain-washed trees and radiant flowers as you wander through the cobbled forested pathways and breathe in the freshness of the air!

Nestled amidst the Himalayan Mountains, the tiny cantonment town of Kasauli evokes the same feelings of emotional contentment that the river Wye had evoked in Wordsworth. Sprawling over the forested slopes, Kasauli is a cosy town where time itself seems to come to a standstill. This quaint secluded place is guaranteed to fill you to the brim with a rare tranquillity. Located in the Solan district of the state of Himachal Pradesh, with winding cobbled paths, enchanting forests, and an untainted beauty, it is the perfect place to escape from the din of everyday city life.

Trivia

British officer Henry Lawrence was the first foreigner to build a house here even before Kasauli was founded. His cottage named Sunnyside stands intact till today.

Indulgence

When in Kasauli, witness the stunning sunset at Sunset Point and trek along the famous trails of Kalka, Garkhal and Jabli. The cable car ride on the Timber Trail will allow you a breathtaking view of the scenic landscape and so will paragliding from Dagshai. Relish the locally brewed wines at Kasauli Brewery and buy specialty products like designer ceramics and antique jewellery from the stores at Lower Mall Road.

Best time to visit

April to June is the perfect time to seek a cool refuge in Kasauli from the Indian summers. In the winter months, the small town turns into a chilly paradise and often experiences snowfall in late December and January. September to November is a splendid time to plan a trip to the immaculate town of Kasauli to soak up the winter sun and have fun bonfire nights!

Monkey Point

Kasauli

The hilltop is believed to have been touched by Lord Hanuman while fetching the Sanjeevani, accounting for the unusual foot-like indentation. Famously known as ‘Tapp’s Nose’, the hilltop sports a tiny Hanuman temple which is a favourite haunt of the neighbouring monkeys.

Good To Know

Being the highest point of Kasauli, it boasts a breathtaking view of the cities of Chandigarh, Kalka, and Panchkula. On a cloudless day, you might catch a glimpse of the mighty Sutlej!

Gurkha Fort

Kasauli

The Gurkha Fort in Subathu is a delightful amalgamation of history and the enriching beauty of nature. The fortification was established by Chief Amar Singh Thapa to strengthen the Gurkhas against the British Army. Though the Gurkhas suffered defeat, the fort still tells stories of their bravery. It is 16 km from Parwanoo and is now a major cantonment of the Indian Army.

Good To Know

The fort houses 180-year-old cannons that were used in those wars and are of immense historic significance.

Festivals

Kasauli

Every July, Kasauli prepares for the grand festivity of Sari, a jolly potpourri of cultural activities. In winter Kasauli decks up for the three-day Renuka Fair celebrated after the harvesting season. While Lohri celebrates the harvest season, Basant Panchami marks the spring season. The memories of Kasauli draped in festive finery are something to hold on to for life.

Good To Know

The Sari Festival attracts a huge number of tourists with its thrilling event of buffalo fighting.

Nahri Temple

Kasauli

Set in the tiny village of Nahri, this 150-year-old temple is devoted to the Goddess of Shakti, Durga and Lord Shiva. With impressive idols of the deities, several pilgrims visit to witness the esteemed celebrations of Dussehra and Shivratri here.

Good To Know

Glowing white in the sunshine and known as ‘Jantar Mantar’ and ‘Choo Mantar Mahadev Temple’, it is an enchanting monument complete with a century-old bauri flowing with clean water making the visit a beautiful spiritual one.

Baptist Church

Kasauli

The Baptist Church is a prime example of the British colonial architecture of Kasauli. Dating back to 1932, the building is a curious blend of Indian and Gothic styles of architecture and has been popular with tourists for the longest time.

Good To Know

Though its internal furnishings were cruelly ravaged by a fire in 2008, it has not hindered the flow of tourists who still travel to this holy spot to pay their respects.

FAQs

Why should I visit Kasauli?

Opposed to popular belief, you should visit the Kasauli to experience the rugged forest life and not just to spot the famed royal Bengal tigers. Though there are about 96 of them including cubs, there haven’t been many regular sightings which is why it can be quite disappointing if you spend your entire visit fixated on the same. On the contrary, cruising the delta and taking morning boat rides in the narrow canals will allow you a surreal experience of nature by spotting rare species of medicinal plants, the charming Sundari trees all around and rare birds and animals of the wild. Visit the delta for itself and let the tigers be a bonus to your thrilling trip here.

Is it safe to travel to Kasauli?

Yes, it is safe provided you stay within the protected areas of the forest. Travelling in small groups will allow you to venture deeper into the forest where bigger boats and cruises cannot enter. However, venturing on your own is very dangerous as the forest is impenetrable in some parts that are better left untraversed without supervision and special protection.

Where to stay in Kasauli?

The stay at Kasauli is sure to add to your forest experience. If you enjoy the thrill of camping then you should opt for camps beside watchtowers or resorts which provide camping facilities. If you prefer to have a more relaxed vacation and enjoy luxuries in the mangrove forest then the Tiger Camp Resort, Hotel Sonar Bangla, Tiger Roar Resort and Tora Eco Resort are options that will delight you.

How long does it take to tour Kasauli and what are the top sites to visit there?

To have a relaxed tour of the mangrove forest and visit all the popular sites would take about 3 to 4 days. Some of the top sites to be visited are the Kasauli National Park, Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary, Sudhanyakhali and Sajnekhali Watch Towers, Bhagabatpur Crocodile Project, Netidhopani and Kanak Islands among others. Crocodiles, birds, an age-old temple and the ferocious tigers can all be found in these places along with sightings of more of the wild.

What should be carried while travelling to Kasauli?

You must carry your Id proof while travelling to Kasauli. Cash in small denominations should also be taken as there are no ATMs inside the forest. Mosquito repellants and other ointments that will keep the insects at bay along with comfortable clothes suitable for the weather you will be travelling in are also advisable to carry to have the desired forest experience.

How to travel to Kasauli from Kolkata?

You can begin your journey from Kolkata at Sealdah Station. The train will take you to Canning in about 1 hour 30 minutes. From this point on there are multiple routes you can take to get to the isles of Kasauli. A bus or car can be taken from Canning to Godakhali, Sonakhali, Koikhali or Jharkhali depending on where you have chosen to stay for your trip. From the ferry ghat of these spots, you need to take a motor boat or launch to venture into the heart of the mangrove forest. If you’re more comfortable travelling by bus then you can also board one from Esplanade to reach Canning and follow the same route from then onward. Since the Kasauli mainly consist of a set of islands, one of which you’ll be staying at, riverine transport is the only way to travel around the place which has a unique thrill of its own.
