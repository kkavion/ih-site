Lonavala

Lonavala's serene surroundings and misty clouds will elevate you to the ethereal.

The hilly town of Lonavala is a natural haven for city dwellers wanting to spend the weekend enwrapped in lush greens and misty clouds. The rough off roads steeping upwards the hill create a scarf for the hills. Here you can trek, camp and indulge in thrilling adventure sports. The air is clean and fresh and instantly rejuvenates your body when it fills the lungs. In monsoon, the water pours down through the tracks rinsing your feet with every step you mount. The trees wreathe a symphony of green harmony, as a beat to every step you take.

Trivia

Dubbed the Jewel of the Sahaydri Range, Lonavala was first discovered by Sir Elphinstone, governor of Bombay in 1811 AD.

Best time to visit

The winter months from November to February bestow a romantic atmosphere and your visits to the caves and forts will be smoked in a misty lustre. During the monsoon months from June to September, the Kataldhara waterfalls get their fill and rappelling is available. The forests and caves are freshly washed and make a splendid scene.

Indulgence

When in Lonavala, submerge yourself in the scenic views from Tiger's Leap, Lion's Point and imbibe the serenity of the Lonavala Lake and waterfall. Trek up to the Lohagad and Rajmachi Forts and uncover the ancient ruins of the Karla and Bhaja Caves for a trip back in time while munching on some sweet chikkis. For some thrilling rides, visit the Della Adventure Park!

Summer March - May

Hot and humid on some occasions and getting more hotter in recent days due to global warming, the temperature is usually mild. The summers see clear blue skies with thin streaks of cirrus clouds. The sun is bright and fresh giving delightful happiness that stays on the faces of the wildflowers.

Good To Know

Wearing light cotton clothes makes trekking and exploring the area more pleasant. Be sure to carry an umbrella to shield yourself from the summer sun!

Monsoon June - September

Windy freshness that breezes away all the weariness is the general nature of the monsoons in Lonavala. The occasional showers put the birds in a musical mood and their opera is heard operating in the seventh realm of the heavens. The thin rays of sunlight streak down through the leaves of tall trees. They shine like yellow rainbows with white wings.

Good To Know

Lonavala receives quite a strong burst of rainfall but is the best time to visit as it appears the most ethereal with the hills looking greener and the waterfalls and dams being replenished.

Good To Know

After a ride through the clouds, you can dismount on the solid station of the airport. If you are coming from out of station then you will have to get off at either the Pune International Airport or the Shivaji International Airport and travel by road to Lonavala. You can also opt for the seaplane route at the Juhu and Pawan Dam which is situated around 14km from Lonavala.

Railway Mumbai

The railway tracks speed through the farmlands of green paddy with pink and white spots and blushes of poppies and seasonal flowers. This route goes to Lonavala from Pune and takes around 2 hours. If you are going to hear the 'building-building' of the metal bridges shout ‘joy' when your train presses against its shoulders and over the rivers then you will have to take the line from Khopoli bus station that begins at Mumbai.

FAQs

Why should I visit Lonavala?

Opposed to popular belief, you should visit the Lonavala to experience the rugged forest life and not just to spot the famed royal Bengal tigers. Though there are about 96 of them including cubs, there haven't been many regular sightings which is why it can be quite disappointing if you spend your entire visit fixated on the same. On the contrary, cruising the delta and taking morning boat rides in the narrow canals will allow you a surreal experience of nature by spotting rare species of medicinal plants, the charming Sundari trees all around and rare birds and animals of the wild. Visit the delta for itself and let the tigers be a bonus to your thrilling trip here.

Is it safe to travel to Lonavala?

Yes, it is safe provided you stay within the protected areas of the forest. Travelling in small groups will allow you to venture deeper into the forest where bigger boats and cruises cannot enter. However, venturing on your own is very dangerous as the forest is impenetrable in some parts that are better left untraversed without supervision and special protection.

Where to stay in Lonavala?

The stay at Lonavala is sure to add to your forest experience. If you enjoy the thrill of camping then you should opt for camps beside watchtowers or resorts which provide camping facilities. If you prefer to have a more relaxed vacation and enjoy luxuries in the mangrove forest then the Tiger Camp Resort, Hotel Sonar Bangla, Tiger Roar Resort and Tora Eco Resort are options that will delight you.

How long does it take to tour Lonavala and what are the top sites to visit there?

To have a relaxed tour of the mangrove forest and visit all the popular sites would take about 3 to 4 days. Some of the top sites to be visited are the Lonavala National Park, Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary, Sudhanyakhali and Sajnekhali Watch Towers, Bhagabatpur Crocodile Project, Netidhopani and Kanak Islands among others. Crocodiles, birds, an age-old temple and the ferocious tigers can all be found in these places along with sightings of more of the wild.

What should be carried while travelling to Lonavala?

You must carry your Id proof while travelling to Lonavala. Cash in small denominations should also be taken as there are no ATMs inside the forest. Mosquito repellants and other ointments that will keep the insects at bay along with comfortable clothes suitable for the weather you will be travelling in are also advisable to carry to have the desired forest experience.

How to travel to Lonavala from Kolkata?

You can begin your journey from Kolkata at Sealdah Station. The train will take you to Canning in about 1 hour 30 minutes. From this point on there are multiple routes you can take to get to the isles of Lonavala. A bus or car can be taken from Canning to Godakhali, Sonakhali, Koikhali or Jharkhali depending on where you have chosen to stay for your trip. From the ferry ghat of these spots, you need to take a motor boat or launch to venture into the heart of the mangrove forest. If you're more comfortable travelling by bus then you can also board one from Esplanade to reach Canning and follow the same route from then onward. Since the Lonavala mainly consist of a set of islands, one of which you'll be staying at, riverine transport is the only way to travel around the place which has a unique thrill of its own.
