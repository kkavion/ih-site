Khandala

Escape the monotonous city life in the long, meandering streets, towering greenery, historical sites, and scenic beauty of Khandala.

A narrow road bisects the hills clad in lush greenery. The hilltops seem eager to kiss the never-ending sky as a blanket of mist and matted cloud enwraps their zenith. From a distance, you hear the faint trickle of a waterfall soothing your senses inured to the cacophony of daily life. Maharashtra's Khandala has often been the respite for many locals. Equipped with versatile hills and sprightly waterfalls, this town is apt for a weekend getaway. Indulgent, affordable, and accessible Khandala lets you enjoy the small town pleasures. You can trek through the trails of The Tiger's Leap and Duke's Nose and explore Buddhist cave temples, forts, and British vestiges.

Trivia

This hilly town made it to pop culture with the song “Aati Kya Khandala” from Amir Khan’s popular movie Gulam.

Indulgence

Visit the Bhaja caves to soak in the calmness and trek through lush greenery in the trails of Tiger’s Valley and Duke’s Nose. Drive around town to let the undisturbed strains of nature seep into you and do not forget to carry chikki, the candy-bar that is the perfect combination of gooey and crunchy back home.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Khandala is the monsoon months. The dry and heaving landscape is spirited by the rains. The waterfall and the lakes are shot back to life- the rains like epinephrine to the pulsating heart. A warm cup of chai or a bowl of Maggi and you are all set to enjoy the Khandala.

Legacy of the World's Largest Mangrove

Khandala, West Bengal 743370

Until the nineteenth century when the British mapped the landscape, large parts of the forest remained uncharted, unexplored, and enshrouded in woody darkness, large parts of it were declared as reserve forests. Slowly the Khandala you know today emerged as a wildlife sanctuary in 1977 and then as a national park in 1984.

Good To Know

Today you can boast about visiting a world heritage site as declared by UNESCO and happily look forward to spotting Royal Bengal Tigers behind the billowy curtains of mangroves!

The culture of the Khandala is exceptionally beautiful for their sense of unity and courage. As the local inhabitants head into the dangerous depths of forest and sea their lives are shaped by the necessity for survival. Forest deities and spirits that you hear of in folklore and legends are ardently worshipped along with Bonobibi, the forest goddess, who is the chief protector of these lands. The desire and need for divine sanction prompt the residents to worship deities like Manosa Devi, Dakshini Rai, and Ghazi Peer.

Khandala is well known for seafood due to the abundance of water around. Fish, prawns, and even crabs are freshly caught and resplendently presented in a variety of delicious recipes. The mouth-watering Hilsa fish with its tantalizing aroma and the special prawn preparation called Chingri Malaikari which is buttery soft and packed to the brim with flavor are smashing hits in the Khandala. Stalls on the mainland also have handcrafted local sweets that celebrate honey- another local produce skillfully obtained by the locals.

Railway Khandala

The Khandala railway station connects the town to several other places like Pune, Lonavala, Mumbai and Solapur. Daily and weekly trains facilitate communication and transportation. The train rides from the aforementioned places take about 2 to 3 hours. This option is completely viable for those short weekend trips with your buddies. The journeys let you enjoy the pleasures of a train ride without fatiguing you so it is a win-win situation for all.

Roadway State Busus

Khandala has a well-connected network of roads. The Mumbai-Pune expressway runs through the town and facilitates transportation. The Maharashtra State Transport plies government buses to and from Khandala. Besides, there are several other buses provided by travel agencies that you can avail of. You can also road trip to Khandala from neighbouring cities like Pune, Lonavala and Mumbai within 2 hours. The roads are quite motorable and the adjoining scenic beauty, quite tempting. So, whether you’re travelling solo or with your company, buses are not a bad idea especially if this is a backpack trip.

FAQs

What to carry to Khandala?

If you’re visiting Khandala during monsoons, carry appropriate footwear, umbrellas and raincoats. Be well equipped to deal with the rains.

What are the drinking laws in Khandala?

Khandala is located in the state of Maharashtra where the legal age of drinking is 25 years. Also, booze tends to be expensive here.

For how long should I stay in Khandala?

A weekend or two days should be enough the tour Khandala. Drive or walk around the town a little bit and definitely visit the Tiger’s Valley, Nagphani and the Bhaja Caves. Go trekking or just relax and soak in hilly airs.

Is Khandala safe?

Khandala is safe with low criminal activities and reports. There are the occasional cases of inconveniences but nothing too serious to frighten you.

Who should tag along on a Khandala trip?

Go solo, with your friends or partners. There aren’t that many things to do in Khandala so it might not entertain your kids all that much.
