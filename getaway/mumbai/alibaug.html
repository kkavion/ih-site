Alibaug

Resplendent with sandy beaches, crystal blue waters, and abundant fresh air, located in the heart of nature, Alibaug is the perfect weekend getaway.

Stretches of land with elegant temples here and there coupled with coconut groves alongside tranquil beaches, contain the perfect solace you are in search for. Alibaug brims with possibilities besides offering a striking serenity. While experiencing gusts of cool wind falling on your face, it will give you a chance to enjoy sunsets and silences. Besides, getting an adventurous thrill of water sports, you can readily explore the captivating fauna of Kanakeshwar Forest. To get a taste of nature in its absolute bliss, Alibaug should be a must visit place on your bucket list!

Indulgence

Alibaug is such a place that it can be visited anytime to spend a pleasurable weekend. The scene is mostly always splendid. However, if you wish to visit it at a time when its most suitable to Indian weather conditions, winter would be your window. The atmosphere is cool and will allow you to explore the area without becoming a perspiring mess.

Best time to visit

Indulging in water sports and exploring the wonderful creatures of nature in the Kanakeshwar Forest is a must. Kihim Beach, Alibaug Beach, Kanakeshwar Devasthan Temple, Kolaba Fort and Hanuman Temple are some of the famous destinations to enjoy. If you wish to travel a little further, you can also visit Murud and Janjita Fort.

Trivia

Alibaug is a loved destination for eminent personalities like Shah Rukh Khan, Sunil Gavaskar and Sachin Tendulkar who have their own bungalows there.

Khanderi & Underi Islands

Maandwa, Thal, Maharashtra

The islands which are now bustling tourist spots, were once used as fortified observatories to keep a check on the Murud-Janjira Fort. The story of the Siddis at Murud Janjira being kept under close supervision dates back to 1600 C.E. The fort has not succumbed to dilapidation, and most of it remains intact till the present day. The structure is largely a lighthouse, which is used to date for navigation purposes, and is a splendour to intake.

Good To Know: You can explore the islands and its forts by taking a ferry from the port that is only 2.8 km away from Kihim Beach.

Kanakeshwar Forest

Maharashtra

With its poignant lush greenery, Kanakeshwar forest is renowned for the overflow of flora and fauna. Besides, a melange of animals like snakes, panthers, leopards and wild boars are found here. Kanakeshwar Forest is an absolute stressbuster in ways inexplicable. There are guides and gunmen for hire, to cater to the needs of tourists who desire a safe enjoyable experience.

Good To Know: The forest is full of poisonous snakes and hence, precautions while travelling within it are more than mandatory.

Kanakeshwar Devasthan Temple

Kankeshwar Mandir, Alibag, Maharashtra 402201

Kanakeshwar is a prime spot for tourist beeline in Alibaug. The Kanakeshwar Devasthan Mandir which is dedicated to Lord Shiva, is located at a distance of 13 km from Alibaug. It is a spectacular experience from the top and is visited by both pilgrims and tourists alike. There is another temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, within the premises. Both attract tourists from every part of the country, all year round.

Kolaba Fort

Alibag, Maharashtra 402201

The 300-year-old Kolaba Fort was once a chief base for the naval forces. There are old temples, significant walls, artifacts, cannons, and carvings of animals and birds among several other possessions within the boundaries of the fort. Situated in the sea, Kolaba Fort is a protected monument under the Archaeological Survey of India.

Good To Know: The Ganesha Mandir inside built by Rahoji Angre is revered, and still worshipped with offerings, by the local fishermen.

Murud-Janjira Fort

Murud, Raigad, Maharashtra

The turquoise waters glistening like a giant mirror owing to the golden sunshine is what Murud-Janjira Fort is surrounded by. Emperor Malik Amber was behind the foundation of this magnificent monument which has its gate of entrance standing proud and sturdy with a carving of a tiger trapped by six majestic elephants. This fort is a tall, strong remnant of the glorious past it had been associated with. This spot is a must, for the grandeur of it is bound to leave you overwhelmed.

Good To Know: The fort is only an hour and a half away from Alibaug.

Waterway By Boat

Alibaug is connected to Mumbai, as well as Bhaucha Dhaka via waterways. The services are mostly time bound, operational within the hours of 6:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. From both the places, there are speedboats running on this route connecting Mumbai to Mandwa, and Bhauvha Dhaka to Rewas. FAQs

Which is a better place to visit, Old Digha or New Digha?

The choice really does lie with the visitor because preferences matter. While Old Digha’s spaced-out hotels and calm shores are perfect for those on a honeymoon, New Digha’s sandy shores, proximity to the park and aquarium and star-rated hotels make it frequently visited as well. Since they’re only 3kms apart, it’s possible to experience both the calm and the bustle.

Which are the top beaches of Digha?

The Old and New Digha beaches along with Udaipur and Shankarpur Beaches are some of the cleanest in West Bengal and offer a charm of their own. Whether you seek seclusion in the waters in a romantic getaway or the classic sandy shores, both can be found on these beaches. The food stalls and water sports available only make the whole experience more exciting.

Are water sports available in Digha?

They are available in abundance on all the beaches, especially the most frequented New Digha Beach. The stalls provide you with equipment and assistance for any activity of your choice. You can be sure to have your enjoyment multiplied with the dash of adrenaline this will bring you. Wearing suitable clothes that won’t wear you down with their weight once wet or fly about if you’re in the air is highly advisable as that makes the experience all the more pleasant.

Which are the best places to eat in Digha?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

What is the fastest route to Digha from Kolkata?

The fastest way to travel to Digha is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 33 minutes to get there but the travel can be a bit pricey, ranging from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000. Cheaper options will take you longer to reach Digha but the journey will be equally exciting if you’re surrounded by your loved ones. You can take a train from Howrah Station and reach in 3 hours 25 minutes, tickets will be priced anywhere between Rs 150 and Rs 850. Buses from Esplanade will take you there in 4 hours 15 minutes and can charge you Rs 150 to Rs 350.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
