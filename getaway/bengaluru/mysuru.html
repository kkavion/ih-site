Mysuru

With its majestic Mysore Palace, grand festivals, splendid hills, and scenic lakes, Mysore is the perfect city to feel royal and luxurious.

The cultural capital of the south and the city of palaces, Mysore is famed for its magnificent heritage and traditions that have been passed down through the centuries. Here mythology and history become one with the city being the grounds on which Goddess Chamundi is said to have slain the demon Mahishasura. The perfect blend of time-honoured customs and contemporary lifestyle, its enormously regal palaces, indigenous crafts, colourful temples, vividly attractive local markets, and delicious food will easily overwhelm you with awe. A visit to the quiet lakes, vast hills, and sanctuaries can truly make you feel in unison with nature.

Trivia

This city of glittering royal heritage and a popular tourist destination, was once the capital of the Wodeyar Dynasty around seven thousand years ago.

Best time to visit

The great Dusshera festivities of Mysore make October to February the perfect time for you to visit. You get to indulge in the vibrant celebrations of Diwali with all its visual extravagance of lights and fire-crackers and the period is apt for adventures like trekking, skydiving, and camping. With occasional showers and beautiful skies, it makes for ideal visits to viewpoints and lakes.

Indulgence

Visit the Mysore and Jaganmohan Palaces to witness their extravagance firsthand, along with the temple cities of Ranganathaswamy Srirangapatna and Nanjanagud. The glassy lakes of Karanji, Kukkarahalli and Blue Lagoon are the perfect places to see a beautiful sunset. Do remember to purchase the famous silk of Mysore and try the delicacy of Mysore Pak in great abundance!

Summer Mid-March to May

Summer season lasts from around mid-march to May and the city experiences a moderately warm climate ranging between 22°C to 39°C. The days are sunny and bright and sometimes stifling, but the evenings are pleasant and cool sometimes accompanied by soft breezes.

Good To Know: If you do happen to be around Mysore during the summers, the evening is perfect for boat rides in lakes and walks in parks or simply spending time with your companion over a cup of coffee.

Monsoon June - September

Monsoons last from around June to September, even though traces of showers are seen from late May itself. The nights can get quite cold and may require you to cover up. If you do happen to be a pluviophile the rains in Mysore are one of a kind and you are sure to enjoy it.

Good To Know: It receives around 667.2mm of rainfall every year and while it is not suggestible to go sightseeing at such a time, it is also a perfect time for you to see the energetic waters in lakes and rivers and the lush green trees on hills and valleys.

Airway Mysore Airport

Mysore airport is about 10kms from the city and TrueJet connects it with Chennai and Belgaum. IndiGo connects it to Hyderabad and Alliance Air connects it to Goa, Kochi, and Bangalore. However, for international tourists, the Kempegowda International Airport or the Kannur International Airport can be availed. Buses or trains can then be taken from there to reach the city.

Railway Mysuru Junction

Mysore is mostly connected to Bangalore, Mangalore, and Chamarajanagar, through electrified single tracks. Some of the trains like Shatabdi Express, Ashokpuram, Chamarajapuram, and Mysore Express are superfast trains in and out of the city. The city has two junctions that are connected to Chamarajanagara, which are visited for religious reasons. The stations are under the jurisdiction of the South Western Railway Zone and one can take a tour of the museum adjacent to Mysore Junction while waiting for their trains.

Roadway City Bus

Buses are available frequently to and from the city. The NH-212 is connected to a bifurcating road near the border town Gundlupet which leads to Kerela and Tamil Nadu. Mangalore is reachable through the NH-275, and the state Highway 17 connects Mysore to Bangalore. You can drive in a car or book tickets for luxury buses and enjoy the greenery stretching along. Driving to hill stations or temple towns by buses or hired cars is quite refreshing since the road winds around hills. There are food stops on the way so you can even admire the peacefulness of the highway with some grub.

FAQs

Why should I visit Mysuru?

Opposed to popular belief, you should visit the Mysuru to experience the rugged forest life and not just to spot the famed royal Bengal tigers. Though there are about 96 of them including cubs, there haven't been many regular sightings which is why it can be quite disappointing if you spend your entire visit fixated on the same. On the contrary, cruising the delta and taking morning boat rides in the narrow canals will allow you a surreal experience of nature by spotting rare species of medicinal plants, the charming Sundari trees all around and rare birds and animals of the wild. Visit the delta for itself and let the tigers be a bonus to your thrilling trip here.

Is it safe to travel to Mysuru?

Yes, it is safe provided you stay within the protected areas of the forest. Travelling in small groups will allow you to venture deeper into the forest where bigger boats and cruises cannot enter. However, venturing on your own is very dangerous as the forest is impenetrable in some parts that are better left untraversed without supervision and special protection.

Where to stay in Mysuru?

The stay at Mysuru is sure to add to your forest experience. If you enjoy the thrill of camping then you should opt for camps beside watchtowers or resorts which provide camping facilities. If you prefer to have a more relaxed vacation and enjoy luxuries in the mangrove forest then the Tiger Camp Resort, Hotel Sonar Bangla, Tiger Roar Resort and Tora Eco Resort are options that will delight you.

How long does it take to tour Mysuru and what are the top sites to visit there?

To have a relaxed tour of the mangrove forest and visit all the popular sites would take about 3 to 4 days. Some of the top sites to be visited are the Mysuru National Park, Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary, Sudhanyakhali and Sajnekhali Watch Towers, Bhagabatpur Crocodile Project, Netidhopani and Kanak Islands among others. Crocodiles, birds, an age-old temple and the ferocious tigers can all be found in these places along with sightings of more of the wild.

What should be carried while travelling to Mysuru?

You must carry your Id proof while travelling to Mysuru. Cash in small denominations should also be taken as there are no ATMs inside the forest. Mosquito repellants and other ointments that will keep the insects at bay along with comfortable clothes suitable for the weather you will be travelling in are also advisable to carry to have the desired forest experience.

How to travel to Mysuru from Kolkata?

You can begin your journey from Kolkata at Sealdah Station. The train will take you to Canning in about 1 hour 30 minutes. From this point on there are multiple routes you can take to get to the isles of Mysuru. A bus or car can be taken from Canning to Godakhali, Sonakhali, Koikhali or Jharkhali depending on where you have chosen to stay for your trip. From the ferry ghat of these spots, you need to take a motor boat or launch to venture into the heart of the mangrove forest. If you're more comfortable travelling by bus then you can also board one from Esplanade to reach Canning and follow the same route from then onward. Since the Mysuru mainly consist of a set of islands, one of which you'll be staying at, riverine transport is the only way to travel around the place which has a unique thrill of its own.
