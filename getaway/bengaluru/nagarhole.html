Nagarhole

A splendid rendezvous of wilderness with serenity, Nagarhole National Park is a magnificent cluster of untouched natural elements in their best form.

The gurgling roar of the Bengal Tiger, an elephant enjoying amidst the lush forest, or the Ghost of Kabini (black panther) eerily eyeing you from a distance not so far away are sights common to Nagarhole. Sharing boundaries with Bandipur Tiger Reserve and Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, it is separated from the two by the majestic Kabini Reservoir, which in itself is a sight to behold. It houses the King Cobra, Indian Rock Python, Russel’s Viper, tortoises, and even monitor lizards. Cloaked with a quilt of green and peace, Nagarhole welcomes the seekers of solitude with open arms and a warm heart.

Trivia

Known to be the erstwhile hunting ground for the rulers of the Wodeyar dynasty, it derives its name from words in Kannada that mean snake and stream due to its many serpentine streams.

Indulgence

Hop onto the back of an elephant to spot rare birds and take the boat safari to go deeper into the parts of the jungle that may not be covered in a jeep safari, to explore the Kabini River. Countless birds, marsh crocodiles resting on the shore, and playful otters make up the scenery. Visit also the gurgling Iruppu Falls and Kerala’s Wayanad Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary.

Best time to visit

The rocky terrain results in the wheels of the safari jeep bouncing and rolling as the animals peek and pop amidst the leaves, Nagarhole is full of surprises. Winter is the best time to visit since the safari and other activities can be enjoyed to their fullest, with a comfortable ambience to keep you hyped up for more. The flora of the park comes out fresh and welcoming for the visitors of Nagarhole during this time.

Legacy of the World's Largest Mangrove Water Sports

Nagarhole, West Bengal 743370

Until the nineteenth century when the British mapped the landscape, large parts of the forest remained uncharted, unexplored, and enshrouded in woody darkness, large parts of it were declared as reserve forests. Slowly the Nagarhole you know today emerged as a wildlife sanctuary in 1977 and then as a national park in 1984.

Good To Know

Today you can boast about visiting a world heritage site as declared by UNESCO and happily look forward to spotting Royal Bengal Tigers behind the billowy curtains of mangroves!

The culture of the Nagarhole is exceptionally beautiful for their sense of unity and courage. As the local inhabitants head into the dangerous depths of forest and sea their lives are shaped by the necessity for survival. Forest deities and spirits that you hear of in folklore and legends are ardently worshipped along with Bonobibi, the forest goddess, who is the chief protector of these lands. The desire and need for divine sanction prompt the residents to worship deities like Manosa Devi, Dakshini Rai, and Ghazi Peer.

Nagarhole is well known for seafood due to the abundance of water around. Fish, prawns, and even crabs are freshly caught and resplendently presented in a variety of delicious recipes. The mouth-watering Hilsa fish with its tantalizing aroma and the special prawn preparation called Chingri Malaikari which is buttery soft and packed to the brim with flavor are smashing hits in the Nagarhole. Summer March - May

The high temperature dries up waterholes, making the perplexed wild animals sneak out on a quest for food and water. This factor contributes to making summer, a premier time to witness what may not be seen otherwise. Days are bright and evenings are pleasant, often chilling with the breeze thanks to the river Kabini. Safaris and outdoor activities are enjoyable during this season.

Good To Know

Sun protection gears should be availed to make the most of the months of April-May. Dark coloured and heavy clothes are not advisable. You can savour the sight of animals roaming freely, and the assortment of flora around.

Monsoon June - September

Monsoon at Nagarhole is something that is as wild as the animals present in it. Showers hit the national park right after summer and are fearfully unpredictable. With an average rainfall of about 1,500 mm, the months of June to September aren’t generally suitable for planning a holiday at Nagarhole.

Good To Know

The park, or precisely, certain areas of the park remain closed during this time, but contrary to puddling the national park at every tiny step, these very monsoons change the Iruppu Falls into something heavenly splendid.

Airway Mysore Airport

Airports at Bengaluru, Mysore and Calicut serve to be the airbases in somewhat proximity to Nagarhole, with Mysore Airport at a distance of 98 km from the park. Due to the park being not exactly where the airports are located, tourists may hire a car or book bus seats to commute to Nagarhole. This is supposed to take around 1.5 hours from Mysore, 4.5 hours from Calicut, and almost 6 hours from Kempegowda International Airport. Domestic airlines operating include those of Indigo, Air Asia, and Spicejet, with Indigo having the maximum number of flights to Bengaluru.

Railway Mysore Junction

The nearest broad-gauge railway station, Mysore Junction is connected to almost the entire country via innumerable trains. Among them, some of the major routes include those of New Delhi, Howrah Junction, Hyderabad Nizamuddin, Mumbai Central, and Patna Junction. Tuticorin Express, Shatabdi Express, Tippu Express, and Chamundi Express are some of the 172 operational trains to and from Mysore Junction. Being at a distance of 90 km, cars and buses may be hired by those willing to commute to Nagarhole. The railway station at Mahe is one located close by, with a lesser number of trains.

Roadway State Owned Buses

Nagarhole National Park is excellently connected by the roadways. The park falls on the Mysore-Ooty highway and that being a nationally acclaimed route, it has numerous modes of road commutation operated by the state as well as the private travel agencies. One Rajhamsa bus is run by Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) from Bengaluru to Kutta, which is around 7 km from Nagarhole. Apart from this, the average duration of a bus journey from Mysore to Nagarhole essentially takes around 1.5 hours, owing to the distance of 65 km.

Local Transport Rental

Owing to the muddy and rough terrain within the boundaries of the national park, local modes of transport mostly include the licensed jeeps and the vehicles of the forest authorities posted there. Foreign or outside vehicles are expected to be parked within the stay premises and are generally not allowed movement within the protected area. Traffic is strictly under check and often, prohibited, during monsoon, thanks to it being the mating season, as well as a time of immense natural adversities within the park.

FAQs

How much are the entry fees for the Park?

The entry fees for the visitors are as follows:
Indians – INR 200
Foreigners- INR 1000
Video Camera- INR 1000.

How do I commute to the park from Bengaluru?

Commutation to the park from Bengaluru isn’t a problem at all, for there are cars under the state as well as private organizations, available for booking at pocket-friendly rates. There aren’t many buses operating on the route, but a KSRTC Rajhamsa runs from the city’s main terminus to Kutta, which is 7 kms from Nagarhole.

What is the best time for spotting wild animals at Nagarhole?

The ideal time to visit this park for a worthy experience is that between the months November-February, for the weather remains favourable for the comfort of the tourists, as well as a bonus for rare sightings to be made.

Are there Indian restaurants inside the park premises?

The park premises apart from having the jungle resorts and authority offices, doesn’t have much. However, there are Indian restaurants located outside the park at menial distances, serving delicious food.

How can I book for the safaris?

Bookings for the safaris can be made in both online as well as the offline modes. The park officials can be contacted for the purpose.

What is the rush season at Nagarhole?

Nagarhole mainly faces a rush in bookings regarding both safaris as well as accommodation, during the winter season. This is because the weather is largely ideal for jungle adventure during this time, especially after the adversities of monsoon clear out.

Are there trains connecting Nagarhole?

As of now, there aren’t any trains connecting Ngarhole specifically. However, the nearest Mysore Junction is a central railway station connecting the region to the rest of the country. Cars and buses can be availed from the station to reach Nagarhole.
