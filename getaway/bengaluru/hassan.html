Hassan

Hassan is just the place to visit for its scenic beauty, Hoysala architecture and the calmness it offers.

The picturesque city of Hassan with its panoramic beauty, magnificent Hoysala architecture, typical of Karnataka, manifested in its age-old temples, distinctive culture and crafts combined with the various kinds of cuisines is the perfect place to rejuvenate your soul. The peaceful calmness and tranquility of this place is in perfect harmony with the purity of its atmosphere surrounding the numerous temples and ancient monuments. At a distance of around 187 kms from Bangalore, the city of Hassan, which acquires its name from its presiding Goddess deity Hasanamba meaning 'Smiling Mother' in Kannada, lies sprawlingly in the Malnad and Maidan districts of Karnataka.

Trivia

Hassan is also famously known as the temple architecture capital of Karnataka due to its historic temple towns.

Best time to visit

Winter is the perfect time to get lost in a beautiful place like Hassan. You would need no one, but a heart to feel, a mind to get lost and a cold atmosphere to experience to the fullest. The weather is perfect to scout the temple towns and spend hours gazing at its beauty without feeling even a bit of exhaustion.

Indulgence

In Hassan, visit Bahubali Statue, Rosary Church and Manjarabad Fort. Inspect the exquisite 12th century temples of Koravangla, Kedareshwara, Ishvara and Hoysala. Go trekking in Kabbaligere Hill, rafting and kayaking in the Tungabhadra River. Try the filter coffee, shop for silk, sandalwood and ivory.

Hassan's climate is basically of tropical type. Summer receives more rainfall than winter here. The temperature is almost high on average. Each season has its own colour to present but the city is most favoured for visiting in winters to avoid the scorching heat in summer and the heavy showers in monsoon.

Summer March - May

Summers and bright, sunny and humid which makes sightseeing exhausting. With the temperatures so high and the sun scorching above, it's best to remain indoors for your weekend trip to Hassan in May.

Good To Know

The summers force animals to venture out in search of water, crocodiles and crabs lay drowsily in the muddy cold salt pools so if you can brave the heat and discomfort you may catch some memorable shots on your cameras.

Monsoon June - September

Albeit not the best, Monsoons are a delightful time to visit Hassan if you wish to see the city and the temple towns washed down by the rains and feel the freshness in the air. The green around seems greener and the overall atmosphere of the city is quite charming.

Good To Know

This is a time when nature rejoices as temperatures dip down to 25-30 degrees Celsius. The mangroves and other flora grow abundantly lush and thick and the wildlife also enters their breeding season.

Airway Mysore Airport

There is no airport in Hassan but the nearby airports are Bangalore International Airport and Mysore Airport. Mysore Airport is 106 km away and cater to a lot of the traffic that heads to Hassan. You can book a cab to take you to the main city or even take the bus.

Railway Hassan Junction

Hassan falls under the south-western railway zone of Indian railways. There is a new broad gauge line laid in 2006 for promoting tourism from Shravanabelagola to Bangalore. A number of trains run between Bangalore, Mysore and Hassan connecting them.

Roadway State Owned Buses

There are well maintained roads connecting cities and towns with state-owned buses plying back and forth. NH 48 and NH 206 run through the city, connecting several places of Hassan. There is a huge bus terminus, helping in the operation of public as well as private buses. The fastest way to reach Hassan would be by cab in under 3 hours 15 minutes. Even buses will take you fairly fast and reach you there in 4 hours 15 minutes.

FAQs

Which is a better place to visit, Old Digha or New Digha?

The choice really does lie with the visitor because preferences matter. While Old Digha’s spaced-out hotels and calm shores are perfect for those on a honeymoon, New Digha’s sandy shores, proximity to the park and aquarium and star-rated hotels make it frequently visited as well. Since they’re only 3kms apart, it’s possible to experience both the calm and the bustle.

Which are the top beaches of Digha?

The Old and New Digha beaches along with Udaipur and Shankarpur Beaches are some of the cleanest in West Bengal and offer a charm of their own. Whether you seek seclusion in the waters in a romantic getaway or the classic sandy shores, both can be found on these beaches. The food stalls and water sports available only make the whole experience more exciting.

Are water sports available in Digha?

They are available in abundance on all the beaches, especially the most frequented New Digha Beach. The stalls provide you with equipment and assistance for any activity of your choice. You can be sure to have your enjoyment multiplied with the dash of adrenaline this will bring you. Wearing suitable clothes that won’t wear you down with their weight once wet or fly about if you’re in the air is highly advisable as that makes the experience all the more pleasant.

Which are the best places to eat in Digha?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

What is the fastest route to Digha from Kolkata?

The fastest way to travel to Digha is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 33 minutes to get there but the travel can be a bit pricey, ranging from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000. Cheaper options will take you longer to reach Digha but the journey will be equally exciting if you’re surrounded by your loved ones. You can take a train from Howrah Station and reach in 3 hours 25 minutes, tickets will be priced anywhere between Rs 150 and Rs 850. Buses from Esplanade will take you there in 4 hours 15 minutes and can charge you Rs 150 to Rs 350.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
