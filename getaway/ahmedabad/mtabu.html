Mount Abu

Mount Abu is the mirage of the desert with its rocky surfaces, rivers, waterfalls, lakes and evergreen forests, making it a staggering ecosystem of vibrant colours and landscapes.

Few places in this world invoke an immediate sense of tranquillity and peace. Among them, Mount Abu rises like a whisper of wind on a cold winter morning. From the dazzling waters of Nakki Lake, to the sprawling mountains to the splashes of green from the tall, dense forests, Mt. Abu is a large canvas of contrasting and distinct hues painted with skilled brushes of nature. The hidden cave temples infuse the atmosphere with an aura of divinity while the wildlife and flora leave you astounded. It is not just a place, but a beacon that beckons weary travellers into its arms for an asylum available only in rare places.

Indulgence

Visit Guru Shikhar, Achalgarh Fort and the Dilwara Jain Temples to soak up the atmosphere of Mt. Abu. Trek to Sunset Point, tour the Wildlife Sanctuary and go boating on the Nakki Lake. Try the much- loved staple of daal, baati churma, laal maas, ghewar and malpua. Buy handicrafts, silver jewellery, Bandhej style bedsheets, and silk sarees.

Best time to visit

Winter is the best time to visit Mt. Abu for the atmosphere retains a mild chill that ultimately warms the heart. Various festivals are celebrated and it is also the best time to capture a look at the wildlife and flora of the mountain. Summers are moderate and monsoons bring an ethereal glow to the green forests in the mountains making them a suitable time to visit.

Trivia

With a close mythological reference, Mt. Abu is believed to be the mystical forest of Arbhudha, tying it to the Sages Vashishtha and Vishwamitra.

Dilwara Jain Temples

Delwada, Mount Abu, Rajasthan 307501

Built between the 11th to 13th centuries, the Dilwara Jain Temples are architectural gems with interior arches and detailed carvings on walls that expertly weave a multitude of stories. A visit to Mt. Abu is incomplete without coming in contact with the sacred grounds of these temples.

Good To Know: These temples are located just 2.5 km near Mt. Abu settlement and are five in number, namely the Vimal Vasahi Temple, Lunal Vasahi, Pittalhar Temple, Shri Parshwanath Temple, and Mahavir Swami Temple.

Shri Raghunath Temple

HPV4+52Q, Nakki Lake, Mount Abu, Rajasthan 307501

Resting near the banks of the Nakki Lake, the Shri Raghunath Temple was constructed in the 14th century in honour of Shri Raghunath Ji, who was known to be an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. It is another quintessentially ornate and eye-catching temple, enticing tourists and pilgrims from all over the country.

Good To Know: Located near the famed Toad Rock, it is hard to miss this house of worship that can be visited together with Nakki Lake and Market and Toad Rock.

Guru Shikhar

JQXG+XR8, Uttaraj path, Uttaraj, Rajasthan 307501

The highest peak of Mount Abu, Guru Shikhar, meaning ‘the peak of Guru’, is named after Lord Dattatreya who is believed to be an incarnation of the Holy Trinity. Perched at a height of 1722 m above sea level, the view is almost indescribable, overlooking a pool of vast vibrant forests and the never-ending Aravallis.

Good To Know: You must take a short horse ride to visit the cave shrine dedicated to Dattatreya nestled on the tip of Guru Shikhar as well as another small shrine dedicated to his mother, Anasuya.

Nakki Lake

Nakki Lake, Mount Abu, Rajasthan, 307501, India

Worshipped by locals, the waters of the lake stream with mythical tales of being constructed by Devtas with their nails or nakhi - which lends it its very name. The only artificial lake above a height of 1200 m above sea level in India, it is located near the market as well as a majority of hotels. The calm, opaque surface of the water forms a gilded mirror of the surrounding greenery and mammoth facade of the mountain, with spectacular sunsets.

Good To Know: The lake is host to activities like boating and picnics with family or your loved ones.

Toad Rock

Mount Abu, Rajasthan 307501

On the banks of Nakki Lake, Toad Rock stands as an anomaly, giving insight into the mysterious ways of nature. It derives its name from its peculiar shape resembling a toad perched on the soil. The rock appears like a toad about to jump into the depths of the lake with abandon. This unusual rock shaped by nature like a skilled sculptor’s magnum opus attracts travellers from all around the world.

FAQs

Which is a better place to visit, Old Digha or New Digha?

The choice really does lie with the visitor because preferences matter. While Old Digha’s spaced-out hotels and calm shores are perfect for those on a honeymoon, New Digha’s sandy shores, proximity to the park and aquarium and star-rated hotels make it frequently visited as well. Since they’re only 3kms apart, it’s possible to experience both the calm and the bustle.

Which are the top beaches of Digha?

The Old and New Digha beaches along with Udaipur and Shankarpur Beaches are some of the cleanest in West Bengal and offer a charm of their own. Whether you seek seclusion in the waters in a romantic getaway or the classic sandy shores, both can be found on these beaches. The food stalls and water sports available only make the whole experience more exciting.

Are water sports available in Digha?

They are available in abundance on all the beaches, especially the most frequented New Digha Beach. The stalls provide you with equipment and assistance for any activity of your choice. You can be sure to have your enjoyment multiplied with the dash of adrenaline this will bring you. Wearing suitable clothes that won’t wear you down with their weight once wet or fly about if you’re in the air is highly advisable as that makes the experience all the more pleasant.

Which are the best places to eat in Digha?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

What is the fastest route to Digha from Kolkata?

The fastest way to travel to Digha is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 33 minutes to get there but the travel can be a bit pricey, ranging from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000. Cheaper options will take you longer to reach Digha but the journey will be equally exciting if you’re surrounded by your loved ones. You can take a train from Howrah Station and reach in 3 hours 25 minutes, tickets will be priced anywhere between Rs 150 and Rs 850. Buses from Esplanade will take you there in 4 hours 15 minutes and can charge you Rs 150 to Rs 350.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
