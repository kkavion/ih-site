Kanha

Reverberating with roars of the Royal Bengal Tiger, Kanha National Park is the paragon of scenic beauty for embracing the wilderness like never before.

Begin your day at Kanha by letting the freshness of the thriving bloom and the mellifluous chirps caress all your senses. This sprawling expanse of captivating forest grassland in Madhya Pradesh will revitalize all your senses and awaken them from protracted slumber. It's divided into four zones providing the best sight and snap of exotic wildlife and nature. It's also commendable for conserving the vulnerable rare swamp deer. The Bamni Dadar- sunset point, horseshoe-shaped valley, intrepid safari amidst enticing scenic beauty and diverse thriving wildlife-birds, make Kanha a paragon for an indelible experience.

Indulgence

Visit Bamni Dadar to see a spectacular sunrise and Bandhavgarh National Park to hear the roars of the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger. Experience the night safari in the Khatia buffer zone to get a mesmeric nocturnal sight of the teeming wildlife. Try Bhutte Ki Kees, Rogan Josh, Dal Bafla, and special Lassi. Buy vibrant handicrafts and woodwork.

Best time to visit

Kanha National Park is hoarded with tourists during the winter months that last from October to March. Hours-long safaris would be more enjoyable without having to rub off sweat from your forehead every two minutes. You can relish the post-monsoon new lease of life being bestowed upon the verdure veil of the reserve, topped with the comfort of winter.

Trivia

The verdant bamboo and sal woods, ravines, and grassy meadows were the muse for Rudyard Kipling's renowned novel, “The Jungle Book”.

Kanha Zone

Madhya Pradesh

Being the oldest, Kanha zone is the most renowned safari zone. Situated near Khatia village, this zone is embellished with a mesmeric landscape comprising meadows, valleys, water bodies and it has the largest tiger population of the reserve. Other than its beauty, you will be fascinated to know the tales of ‘Shravan Taal’, known for Shravan Kumar being mistaking killed by King Dasarath and ‘The Schaller Road’ that is dedicated to George Schaller, a writer, conservationist and researcher.

Good To Know: Often reached after traversing over the eroded rocks stacked on the edge of the sands, the beach is a stunning spot for bathing under the sun or in the sea.

Kisli Zone

Madhya Pradesh 481111

Kisli Zone is the oldest as it was a part of the Kanha zone hitherto it was carved out as a separate zone. The renowned Khatia entrance trail lies in the Kisli Zone. The scenic environs and high wildlife populace make it one of the most photogenic zones. With proper safari guide, you might spot Jackals, Gaur, Barasingha, prey-predator brawls or even the sight of mothers nursing the younger ones.

Good To Know: Star of the show is the tiger and if you are lucky enough you might spot an entire fiery-stripped family roaming or devouring their recent kill.

Bandhavgarh National Park

Madhya Pradesh 484661

Add a few more stories to your wildlife archive by extending your itinerary to Bandhavgarh National Park. Summer April - June

The climate of Kanha National Park is extreme, and the summers, sultry. The summer months last from April to June and the temperature may hike up to 43˚C or more at times. May is the warmest month, so if the wrath of the sun doesn't please you much it is better to avoid this month but every con comes with a pro; you can get luring discounts in these months.

Good To Know: Due to dried-up vegetation during summer, it gets relatively easier to spot tigers.

Monsoon July - September

The monsoon months last the long stretch of mid-June to September. Kanha National Park receives an average rainfall of 1800 mm. The heavy rainfall might hinder your plans but the invigorating aura of the park with the fresh spell of the shower is rejuvenating. Albeit the temperature remains moderate, torrential downpour is better to avoid.

Good To Know: The park remains closed during the extreme monsoon months - July to mid-October.

Winter December - February

The chilly winter months ensue with October and spread its nippy shrouds until February. The stretch of October to November is comforting as the extreme hit of cold blows December onwards. The early mornings of November might seem equally chilly but with the subtle scintillating shafts of the sun, the weather becomes pleasant. The temperature of December drops down to 2-3˚C with frosts.

Good To Know: In winter, you will get the inimitable delight of bird watching as hordes of over 300 species of migratory birds flock to Kanha.

FAQs

Which is a better place to visit, Old Digha or New Digha?

The choice really does lie with the visitor because preferences matter. While Old Digha’s spaced-out hotels and calm shores are perfect for those on a honeymoon, New Digha’s sandy shores, proximity to the park and aquarium and star-rated hotels make it frequently visited as well. Since they’re only 3kms apart, it’s possible to experience both the calm and the bustle.

Which are the top beaches of Digha?

The Old and New Digha beaches along with Udaipur and Shankarpur Beaches are some of the cleanest in West Bengal and offer a charm of their own. Whether you seek seclusion in the waters in a romantic getaway or the classic sandy shores, both can be found on these beaches. The food stalls and water sports available only make the whole experience more exciting.

Are water sports available in Digha?

They are available in abundance on all the beaches, especially the most frequented New Digha Beach. The stalls provide you with equipment and assistance for any activity of your choice. You can be sure to have your enjoyment multiplied with the dash of adrenaline this will bring you. Wearing suitable clothes that won’t wear you down with their weight once wet or fly about if you’re in the air is highly advisable as that makes the experience all the more pleasant.

Which are the best places to eat in Digha?

With the place rapidly developing, the eateries and restaurants in Digha are increasing in number and standard. Local food stalls and those by the beach are sure to give you the perfect savouries to snack on while you laze on the beach or indulge in water sports. Other restaurants that will cater to your seafood cravings and match your expectations are The Bite Restaurant, Mahabhoj Restaurant, Restaurant Bhorpet, Aqua Blues and Koshe Kosha. If you’re looking for cheaper options but good quality food, the Bay Cuffe Restaurant and Elgin Restaurant will fit the bill. All in all the food experience in Digha is one to look forward to and delight in.

What is the fastest route to Digha from Kolkata?

The fastest way to travel to Digha is by cab. It takes approximately 2 hours 33 minutes to get there but the travel can be a bit pricey, ranging from Rs 3,000 to Rs 4,000. Cheaper options will take you longer to reach Digha but the journey will be equally exciting if you’re surrounded by your loved ones. You can take a train from Howrah Station and reach in 3 hours 25 minutes, tickets will be priced anywhere between Rs 150 and Rs 850. Buses from Esplanade will take you there in 4 hours 15 minutes and can charge you Rs 150 to Rs 350.

Is any music or dance festival hosted in Digha?

If you enjoy music and dance performances, visiting Digha in December is what will make your holiday even more pleasant. The town celebrates the Digha Beach Festival during this time when a multitude of stalls is set up for the carnival. You can relish the delicious appetizers, play games, or watch the music recital and dance showcase that features performers from all over the country.
