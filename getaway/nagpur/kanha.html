Kanha

Reverberating with roars of the Royal Bengal Tiger, Kanha National Park is the paragon of scenic beauty for embracing the wilderness like never before.

Begin your day at Kanha by letting the freshness of the thriving bloom and the mellifluous chirps caress all your senses. This sprawling expanse of captivating forest grassland in Madhya Pradesh will revitalize all your senses and awaken them from protracted slumber. It's divided into four zones providing the best sight and snap of exotic wildlife and nature. It's also commendable for conserving the vulnerable rare swamp deer. The Bamni Dadar- sunset point, horseshoe-shaped valley, intrepid safari amidst enticing scenic beauty and diverse thriving wildlife-birds, make Kanha a paragon for an indelible experience.

Trivia

The verdant bamboo and sal woods, ravines, and grassy meadows were the muse for Rudyard Kipling's renowned novel, “The Jungle Book”.

Best time to visit

Kanha National Park is hoarded with tourists during the winter months that last from October to March. Hours-long safaris would be more enjoyable without having to rub off sweat from your forehead every two minutes. You can relish the post-monsoon new lease of life being bestowed upon the verdure veil of the reserve, topped with the comfort of winter.

Indulgence

Visit Bamni Dadar to see a spectacular sunrise and Bandhavgarh National Park to hear the roars of the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger. Experience the night safari in the Khatia buffer zone to get a mesmeric nocturnal sight of the teeming wildlife. Try Bhutte Ki Kees, Rogan Josh, Dal Bafla, and special Lassi. Buy vibrant handicrafts and woodwork.

Climatic condition plays a pivotal role in Wildlife tourism. At times, excess rain or heat waves may hamper the tour, or at other times, you might land up in such a season when spotting mammals or birds get knotty. Like most of the woods in Central India, Kanha has an extreme and tropical climate and opens for a visit from 15th October to 30th June, October to March being the peak season.

Summer April - June

The climate of Kanha National Park is extreme, and the summers, sultry. The summer months last from April to June and the temperature may hike up to 43˚C or more at times. May is the warmest month, so if the wrath of the sun doesn't please you much it is better to avoid this month but every con comes with a pro; you can get luring discounts in these months.

Good To Know

Due to dried-up vegetation during summer, it gets relatively easier to spot tigers.

Monsoon July - September

The monsoon months last the long stretch of mid-June to September. Kanha National Park receives an average rainfall of 1800 mm. The heavy rainfall might hinder your plans but the invigorating aura of the park with the fresh spell of the shower is rejuvenating. Albeit the temperature remains moderate, torrential downpour is better to avoid.

Good To Know

The park remains closed during the extreme monsoon months - July to mid-October.

Winter December - February

The chilly winter months ensue with October and spread its nippy shrouds until February. The stretch of October to November is comforting as the extreme hit of cold blows December onwards. The early mornings of November might seem equally chilly but with the subtle scintillating shafts of the sun, the weather becomes pleasant. The temperature of December drops down to 2-3˚C with frosts.

Good To Know

In winter, you will get the inimitable delight of bird watching as hordes of over 300 species of migratory birds flock to Kanha.

Airway Jabalpur & Nagpur Airport

Although there is no direct flight to Kanha National Park, here are some of the nearest airports that you can opt for. Jabalpur airport is one, which is 177 km away and at a distance of 266 km, Nagpur is the nearest airport that is well connected to all the domestic airports of India. You can board any local transport upon reaching the airport.

Railway Jabalpur, Gondia, and Nagpur

Jabalpur, Gondia, and Nagpur are situated at a distance of 177 km, 200 km, and 266 km respectively from the Kanha National Park. These three areas have rail links that are connected with the rest of India. You need to travel for 3 hours to reach Kanha National Park from Jabalpur and it takes even more time, around 5 hours to travel to Kanha Park from Nagpur.

Roadway State Owned Bus

You can board available buses that ply daily from Jabalpur, Raipur, Bilaspur, and Nagpur to Kanha National Park. If you are commencing your journey from Jabalpur then you can get on a bus to reach Mandla and then get on another bus from there to reach Kanha-Kisli. Buses also ply from other places like Indore, Bhopal, etc.

Local Transport Rental

Other than local buses, you can easily hire a cab for commuting in and around Kanha National Park. Cabs and buses are readily available to any point of transit – airport and railway station. Cars can also be hired for your entire trip to the park. If you wish to drive your car to Kanha, that too is possible. Most of the renowned hotels also provide jeeps for a trip to the park.

FAQs

Which river flows through Kanha National Park?

The Kanha Tiger Reserve is situated on the banks of the Banjar River.

What is the distance between Kanha and Bandhavgarh?

By road, the distance between Kanha and Bandhavgarh is 244km.

Is the water at Kanha safe to drink?

Yes, The RO water provided at Kanaha Jungle camp is safe to drink.

Are children allowed on safaris?

Yes, children of all age groups are allowed on the safaris under the supervision of a guardian.

How long should I stay?

At least 3 days are recommended for the best experience.
